SALEM

CAPITAL OF OREGON

The Salem Capital building is home of a gilded statue made of 8 1/2 tons of bronze which is nicknamed the "Oregon Pioneer" or the "Gold Man".

    Facts about Salem:
  • CAPITAL OF OREGON
  • Year Incorporated: November 9, 1857
  • City's Population: 174,365
  • City Classification: Suburban
  • Average Family Income of Portland: $82,450
  • Average Income Compared to State: Salem average income lower than Oregon state

Salem is the capital of Oregon and sits about an hour south from Oregon's biggest city, Portland. Salem sits on the 45th parallel which is half way between the equator and the north pole. The State or Oregon is the largest employer in Salem. The United State's 31st President, Herbert Hoover, lived in Salem for three years.
